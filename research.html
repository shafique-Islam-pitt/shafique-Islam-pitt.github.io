Working Paper

Delivering Degrees: Positive Educational Spillovers from Amazon's Logistics Expansion Working paper. [pdf copy]

  • This paper estimates the causal effect of Amazon fulfillment center entry on local educational attainment, exploiting staggered entry timing across U.S. commuting zones from 2010 to 2023.
  • Amazon's arrival increases bachelor's degree attainment by 0.58 percentage points and master's degree attainment by 0.41 percentage points, with no evidence of the dropout increases that characterize traditional resource booms. Four-year public college enrollment rises by 0.52 percentage points.
  • Two channels drive these effects: rising household incomes relax financial constraints on educational investment, while spillovers to information-intensive establishments and high-skill occupations raise the returns to advanced degrees. Effects are concentrated among men and white students.

Work In Progress

Sampling into Significance: How Random Are Results in Economics Research? With Sonia Ale, Lester Lusher, Huda Osman, and Jacob Stenstrom.

  • Empirical inference assumes that estimators are correct on average, yet researchers typically observe only a single realization from a stochastic data-generating process.
  • Using a setting where repeated draws from the same data-generating process are observable, this project replicates findings from 21 papers in top economics journals and re-estimates each result using 40 independent resamples.
  • Across 3,000 resampled results, t-statistics shrink by 36%, frequently leading to lost significance. Proxies for researcher degrees of freedom and sampling noise predict reduced significance, highlighting how statistical evidence can vary even when research design is fixed.
