Welcome!

I am a third-year PhD student in the Department of Economics at the University of Pittsburgh. My research sits at the intersection of labor economics, development economics, and applied econometrics.

My current work examines how large-scale economic forces, the rise of e-commerce, technological change, and AI adoption, shape labor markets, human capital formation, and inequality. Projects include the educational spillovers of Amazon's logistics expansion, how early warehouse employment affects long-run immigrant and refugee integration, the distributional consequences of AI adoption for the gender wage gap, and how sampling variation shapes statistical significance in published economics research.

I hold an M.A. in Economics from the University of Pittsburgh, an M.A. in Economics from Central Michigan University, and a B.S.S. and M.S.S. in Economics from the University of Dhaka.
