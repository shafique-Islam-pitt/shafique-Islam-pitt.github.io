I am a third-year PhD student in Economics at the University of Pittsburgh. My research focuses on labor economics, development economics, public economics, and applied econometrics.

Before starting my PhD, I completed an MA in Economics at Central Michigan University and earned both my MSS and BSS in Economics from the University of Dhaka. I have also taught economics courses at the University of Pittsburgh, Central Michigan University, and Noakhali Science and Technology University.

My broader work combines policy-oriented economic questions with empirical analysis using large-scale datasets, text data, and time-series methods.

Below you can find my current working paper, ongoing research, and selected publications. My CV contains a fuller record of research, teaching, and previous experience.

Working Paper

Delivering Degrees: Positive Educational Spillovers from Amazon's Logistics Expansion Working paper. [paper link]

  • This paper examines how Amazon fulfillment center expansion affects local educational attainment across U.S. commuting zones between 2010 and 2023.
  • The results suggest positive effects on bachelor's and master's degree attainment through changes in returns to schooling, technology spillovers, and selective in-migration.
  • Submitted to the Society of Labor Economists (SOLE) Conference.
