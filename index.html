Welcome!

I am a third-year PhD student in the Department of Economics at the University of Pittsburgh.

I study questions at the intersection of labor, development, and applied econometrics. My current work examines how large-scale economic forces, such as e-commerce expansion, AI adoption, and labor market shocks, shape educational attainment, immigrant and refugee integration, and wage inequality, particularly for underserved and disadvantaged groups.

I hold an M.A. in Economics from the University of Pittsburgh, an M.A. in Economics from Central Michigan University, and a B.S.S. and M.S.S. in Economics from the University of Dhaka.

You can contact me at shafique.islam@pitt.edu.
