I am a third-year PhD student in Economics at the University of Pittsburgh. My research focuses on labor economics, development economics, public economics, and applied econometrics.

Before starting my PhD, I completed an MA in Economics at Central Michigan University and earned both my MSS and BSS in Economics from the University of Dhaka.

I have teaching experience at the University of Pittsburgh, where I have served as a teaching assistant for Introduction to Microeconomics and Introduction to Macroeconomics. I also previously worked as a Graduate Teaching Assistant at Central Michigan University and as an Assistant Professor and Lecturer at Noakhali Science and Technology University.

My broader work combines policy-oriented economic questions with empirical analysis using large-scale datasets, text data, and time-series methods.
